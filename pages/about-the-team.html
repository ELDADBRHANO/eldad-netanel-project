Black Jazz

Who we ARE?

Black Jazz Records is a record company and label founded in Oakland, California by Eldad Brhano and Netanel yaso. The label was created to promote the talents of young black musicians and singers, and released twenty albums between 1971 until today. The artists who recorded for Black Jazz Records included LIOR AYTEGEV, former bassist for Count SOLOMON AVRHAM and DANIEL MALEDA, and organist/pianist ABAYNECH ASYA, whose four albums were the most successful of any Black Jazz artist.LIOR's wife at the time, LIOR AYTEGEV, sang on his albums; she changed her name to Jean Carne and had a successful solo career as an R & B singer. Singer Kellee Patterson gained notice as the first black Miss Indiana in 1971, before recording her debut album, Maiden Voyage, with Black Jazz Records in 1973. The label was distributed and financed by Ovation Records, a country and western label based in Chicago, which was also founded by Schory.Black Jazz Records was considered at the time to be the first jazz label started by an African American since brothers John and Reb Spikes started Sunshine Records in 1921.
