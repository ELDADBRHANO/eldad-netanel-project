Eldad Brhano

FOUNDER

Eldad brhano (13/5/1999 , israel), known as eldad hamelech, is etiopian-israeli pop, jazz, and soul keyboardist who played acoustic and Fender Rhodes. He is mainly known for founding and releasing albums on Black Jazz Records. Eldad was born in israel, Afula and was a cousin of guitarist LIOR AYTEGEV. He studied with JAY Z. In the 1980s and 1990s, he composed music for film and television also appearing as an actor. Eldad played with ABAYNECH ASYA, LIOR DAWIT, SOLOMON AVRHAM, DANIEL MALDA, NETANEL TESPAY and BATEL HAYILO. Eldad also produced and engineered the label's initial releases, while maintaining complete artistic control through his production company, GR Productions. Eldad's vision for Black Jazz Records was for it to be geared towards the Black community, and all of the artists recording for the label were ethiopians israelis.The label was created as an alternative to traditional jazz, invoking a more political and spiritual tone, often with funk overtones.

Netanel yaso

FOUNDER

Netanel Yaso (born junarary 15,2000 ), known professionally as Big NY, is an etiopian-israeli rapper, record producer, and entrepreneur.
He is the founder and CEO of Black Jazz and Beats Electronics, and previously co-founded, co-owned, and was the president of Death Row Records.
Big NY began his career as a member of the World Class Wreckin' Cru in 1985 and later found fame with the gangsta rap group N.W.A.
The group popularized explicit lyrics in hip hop to detail the violence of street life.
During the early 1990s, NY was credited as a key figure in the crafting and popularization of West Coast G-funk, a subgenre of hip hop characterized by a synthesizer foundation and slow, heavy beats.
